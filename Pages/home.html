The search for a new and environmentally conscious source of energy is ongoing but to keep up with the rising energy demand in today’s market, petroleum continues to be our number one source of energy. North America’s unconventional oil production has been undergoing a steep incline since 2010. The exploitation of this valuable resource comes with a challenge, waste water treatment.

The use of water is paramount in the production of shale products such as oil and gas. In shale production water is injected into wells to fracture shale deposits in order to liberate trapped gases and liquids. Flow back water and water ejected from these wells is highly contaminated with sand, clay, polymers, oil, salt, dissolved solid and even radioactive materials. Waste water treatment is an essential part of this whole process. Unfortunately, because of the new nature of these oil resources not enough research been invested to investigate the best practices in this waste water treatment.

The WTR Technology conference is aimed towards finding a solution to the rising challenges in waste water treatment in the unconventional Oil and gas industry. The conference will focus on bringing together industry and research worldwide to discuss these challenges and establish valuable partnerships. The collaborative event is the first step towards establishing public acceptance for these sources of energy until a new and viable source of energy is established.
